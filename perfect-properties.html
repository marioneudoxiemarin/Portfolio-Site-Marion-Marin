Perfect Properties

The idea of this project is to bring something new to real estate apps. My goal was to find what is useful for users. What could be an asset in searching for a new home to buy? The UI part was very important here to further differentiate this app from many others. I wanted to attract users thanks to useful features as well as by a trustworthy and efficient visual design

My Role

For this project, I was an Interaction Designer, a Visual Designer, an Information Architect, a UX Designer and a User Researcher.

The Challenge

There are many apps for real estate on the market and most are well established. The challenge here was to bring something both new and useful to users. What feature was lacking in other apps? What were the main problems of users?

My Design Process

RESEARCH

Concept

  • Target audience: first time buyers, with middle to upper class salaries, in their 30s/40s, who want to invest for additional income or more financial security
  • Competition: there are a lot of different apps / websites which feature offers of properties to buy or rent
  • Risk: how to establish our app in a market where they are a lot of other similar products
  • Opportunity: focusing on buyers only is different from what is usually offered on similar popular products.

What are The Similar Apps on The Market?

There are lots of different apps providing users with information on houses to buy or rent such as Zillow, Realtor.com and Trulia in the United States. Here is what I found all those had in common during my research:

  • Users can rent or buy on those platforms
  • Maps are a major component of their websites or apps
  • There is a lot of information in their screens which can be overwhelming especially for first-time buyers
  • There is a way to calculate how you afford the property on those apps which is often thought about by users before the actual search with a consultation with advisors, friends and their banks (it then might be a feature to remove here).
  • They offer the possibility to book a visit of the house online which is useful and fast.

User Stories

  • As a user, I want to create a profile containing all my property criteria, so that I am recommended results most relevant to me.
  • As a user, I want to be able to search and filter properties, so that I can find good matches based on my needs.
  • As a user, I want to be able to save or mark properties I am interested in, so that I can easily revisit them.
  • As a user, I want access to as much written and visual information as possible about properties Iâ€™m interested in, so that I can make an informed decision.
  • As a user, I want to be able to contact the right people if I am interested in viewing a property, so that I schedule a viewing.
  • As a user, I want to see how well a property meets my criteria or compares to other properties, so that I can refine my options.

Important Elements of the Design

  • Filtered search (input of criteria is the first step after onboarding and it can later be refined even further)
  • A match percentage for users to see how much properties match their criteria
  • A map to show where the properties are and what's around them (schools, restaurants, groceries store...)
  • The possibility to add a property to the favorites in one click
  • Booking of a visit in a few clicks
  • An overview of the steps to make to sign for a property

USABILITY TESTING

Problems and Solutions

I conducted two rounds of usability testing : one when the first wireframes were finished and and another one when the mid-fidelity ones were done. For the first round of testing, all sessions were conducted online via Skype because of COVID 19. Skype allowed for the participant to share their screen as well as to see their face which is great to see their reactions. For the second round, some sessions were still done on Skype and some were done face to face. For each round, I tested the app with 6 participants. Only the mobile app was tested since I followed a mobile-first approach.

Evolution of the Dashboard Screen

PROBLEM: The users felt the price was not too visible
SOLUTION: The visual part of the main page was rethought with an emphasis on the price (bold and bigger than the rest of the text)

Evolution of the filters screens (left to right)

PROBLEM: The users felt like some filters were not needed at this stage (neighborhood rating, financial filters)
SOLUTION: Based on the users comments, I lowered the number of filters removing the neighborhood and school ratings (which are still visible on the map for each property (see next screens). I also removed the financial aids filters as it seems that the users would prefer external help (bank, friends and family, advisors) to determine if a property is worth the investment.

PROBLEM: It was important for the users to know which kind of parking was available and to know how many cars the garage could fit.
SOLUTION: It was added to the filters.


Evolution of the property description screens (from left to right)

PROBLEM : The users felt there was a lot of information on one page and that the important elements were not highlighted enough.
SOLUTION: The information is now presented on two different screens ("description" and "map"). The "description" screen show the important elements of the property like the filters, a description, the option to book a visit and recommendations. The "map" screen shows the map in a visually more attractive size and on this map, the users can see everything available around the property (restaurants, schools, parks, groceries stores, gyms).

Evolution of the booking screens (from left to right)

PROBLEM: During the testing, it was noted that if a lot of dates and time slots are available, the first version of the design would not be user friendly (it will be a long list of availabilities).
SOLUTION: Now, the user selects the date and a dropdown appears will the time slots available.
