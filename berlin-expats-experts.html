Berlin Expats Experts

As a UX Design student, I was asked to select a project I would work on during the duration of the course. The objective of the project I chose was "enable anyone, anywhere to instantly chat with an expert in virtually any field". I was free to get inspired by this objective and to come up with a concept of my own. I chose to focus myself on a few categories of experts that could help expatriates to settle down in Berlin.

My Role

For this project, I was an Interaction Designer, a Visual Designer, an Information Architect, a UX Designer and a User Researcher.

The Challenge

I had to do all the steps of the project by myself. I had the help of a tutor and mentor but it was still a lot of roles to assume by myself. It took a lot of time but in the end, I learned a lot about UX jobs and about my project.

Tools Used

Adobe XD (wireframes, prototypes, testing)
Zeplin (assets export)

My Design Process

RESEARCH

Concept

  • Target audience: expatriates in Berlin, 18-40 years old, limited German knowledge.
  • Competition: websites for expats and Facebook groups
  • Risk: establish the app as the one trusted tool for expats.
  • Opportunity: no other app with the same concept.

Problem

List of problems established after the interviews:
  • Needing answers fast
  • Needing a variety of contact (calls, appointments, messages)
  • Possibility of getting some help without leaving home or work
  • Not being able to find experts in Berlin who speak our native language
  • Needing to have answers for specific questions without needing a full consultation

What are the similar apps on the market?

To know in what direction to go, I did some research about experts apps already on the market. From this search, I discovered some opportunities to explore:
  • Offer an app for a certain market (for a specific country, specialization etc).
  • The experts should be screened (diplomas, experience)
  • Offer to pay for each booking, no monthly installment

Examples of research on Google Play about experts in Berlin

Our Users and What They Can Do Using The App

I knew my potential users would be expatriates with limited knowledge of German. I needed to talk with expatriates in Berlin to understand what they would expect from an app like mine.
From these interviews, I mapped out the kind of the users I could expect and all the tasks they could do on the app. It also made me refine the type of experts that would be on the app :
  • Translators
  • Doctors
  • Lawyers
  • Legal Advisors

WHO ARE THE USERS?

User Personas

We have two main types of users :
  • A user looking to contact an expert
  • An expert looking to help users by providing answers to questions and appointments


User Journeys

The goals and paths of the two main type of users is different:
  • A user goes to find information about an expert to contact them
  • An expert receives information from the users to act on it.


The Users' Flows

Here you can see two different flows :
  • The user comes on the app to search for information about an expert who could help him solve issues.
  • The expert comes on the app to receive demands and answer them (questions, appointments demands).

The User's Dashboard

The dashboard is the place where the most important information for the user has to be present. I wanted it to be informative and helpful. We created two different dashboards to fit the needs of our two users personas : one for users, one for experts.

  • The search bar was replaced by a search by specialties (main way to search).
  • The search function (by name) was put in the top bar and takes less space.
  • Recommendations are another way to guide the users in their searches. They're based on previous searches for returning users. For new users, they're based on the best rated experts.
  • I made each appointment reminder box more visible and added the possibility to message the expert directly.


Evolution of the User Dashboard Screen

The Expert's Dashboard

The expert's dashboard focuses on :

  • Rating
  • Reviews
  • Upcoming appointments

The experts want to know more about what the users say about them and try to improve their rating as much as possible since it's one of the main filters. That's why it takes such an important part of the screen.

Evolution of the Expert's Dashboard

The Filters

I wanted this part of the journey to be simple and filtered down to the user's needs. I realized that too many filters could be overwhelming.
What I did to solve this :

  • Search filters are seen on a separate screen
  • I reduced the number of filters from 6 to 4 (languages, price, rating, location).
  • he availability filter was redundant as the user chooses available dates in the calendar later on.


Evolution of the Filters

The Expert's Profile

The expert's profile is supposed to convince the user to contact the expert. The first iterations were not communicating this in an efficient manner and the descriptions were not detailed enough. What I did to solve this:

  • Calls to action higher on the screen ("book" and "message" buttons)
  • Prices in the upper part of the screen, before description
  • Description matching filters demands (languages and location clearly stated)


Evolution of the Expert's profile

USABILITY TESTING

Problems and Solutions

I conducted the usability testing at the end of the design flow when I already had created a few iterations of the high fidelity prototype.

  • Type: moderated in person tests
  • Participants: 6 women, ages 26-30, expatriates in Berlin
  • Duration: About 20 minutes per person
  • Equipment: Android Smartphone
  • Flows tested: user and expert flows

Problems

  • Search function with a search bar to be modified to simplify​​​​​​​
  • The filters can be overwhelming
  • Agenda feature to be simplified
  • The ratings should be presented in a different way
  • The notifications icon should be changed
  • Need for more transitions in the booking process

Solutions

  • The search bar was removed in favor of a developing search bar in the top bar.
  • There are now less filters on a separate screen.
  • The calendar was removed and only the appointment boxes remain.
  • Stars now represent the ratings instead of numbers.
  • The notifications icon was changed for a more traditional one.
  • More transitions were added in the booking process to guide the user.
